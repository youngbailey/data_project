Mental Illness in Veterans

in the United States

By BAILEY YOUNG      Oct. 31, 2021

This dataset, also known as the National Veteran Health Equity Report details patterns and rates of heath conditions for vulnerable Veteran groups. It provides comparative information on sociodemographics and rates of diagnosed health conditions in vulnerable groups. The information will allow Veteran Affairs, Veterans, and stakeholders monitor the care Veterans receive and set goals for improving care.

Most Common Illnesses

The National Veteran Health Equity Report focuses on 5 categories of mental illness: anxiety, PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder), substance abuse, serious mental illness, and other mental illnesses. From this data, anxiety is overwhelmingly the most common mental illness among the five with over a million reported cases. This is followed by serious mental illness and PTSD. Of the data, the majority of reported cases come from men.
